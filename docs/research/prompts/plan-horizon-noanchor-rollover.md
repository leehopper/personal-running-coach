# Research Prompt: R-087
# NoAnchor Plan Rollover — What a Rolling Training Horizon Does When It Runs Out of Declared Weeks

Copy the prompt below and hand to your deep research agent.

---

## PROMPT

Research Topic: RunCoach is an AI running coach that maintains an event-sourced training plan across three tiers (macro periodization phases, meso weekly templates, micro daily prescriptions) and now keeps meso+micro detail generated ahead of the runner via a background sweeper, up to the macro plan's declared total length. For a runner training toward a race, the plan's length is anchored to the race date, so "the plan ends" has an obvious, correct answer. For a **NoAnchor plan** — general fitness, no target event or date — there is no such anchor, yet the macro tier still has to declare *some* total length, and the runner keeps training past it. What should happen when a NoAnchor plan's rolling horizon reaches the last week the macro tier declared, and the runner is still training?

## Context

I'm building RunCoach, an AI-powered running coach (.NET 10 / ASP.NET Core backend, event-sourced plan state on Marten/PostgreSQL, Claude Sonnet for coaching reasoning via a versioned-YAML-prompt adapter). The product's core differentiator (`docs/planning/vision-and-principles.md`) is that it's not a static plan generator — it's meant to be "the persistent, adaptive intelligence that... reasons about [the runner's training history] continuously." A plan that goes stale or repeats blindly once it runs off the end of its declared weeks directly undermines that thesis.

### The three-tier plan model (locked, `docs/planning/planning-architecture.md`)

- **Macro** (months/season): a phase schedule with constraints — phase type, week ranges, target weekly volume, intensity distribution, allowed workout types. Implemented as `MacroPlanOutput { TotalWeeks, GoalDescription, Phases[], Rationale, Warnings }`, where each `PlanPhaseOutput` carries a `PhaseType` (currently `Base | Build | Peak | Taper | Recovery` — **note:** the original planning doc names a sixth phase type, "maintenance," for exactly the goalless/rolling case, but no `Maintenance` value exists in the implemented `PhaseType` enum today; confirm whether it should).
- **Meso** (weekly cycles): a weekly slot template per week, generated one `MesoCycleCreated(int WeekIndex, MesoWeekOutput Meso)` event at a time.
- **Micro** (daily prescriptions): generated just-in-time per week via `MicroCycleCreated(int WeekIndex, MicroWorkoutListOutput Micro)`.
- The original planning doc's aspirational framing for goalless runners was: macro "degrades to a simple 'current emphasis' tag (aerobic focus, speed maintenance, strength block) that rotates every 4-8 weeks," and meso "becomes a rolling 4-week block with auto-deload." **This was never implemented as such** — today a NoAnchor plan still gets a concrete LLM-authored `MacroPlanOutput` with a real `TotalWeeks` and a real `Phases[]` array, structurally identical to an anchored plan's. The "rotates every 4-8 weeks" idea was never built. Treat the planning doc as directional intent, not an implemented contract — the research should evaluate it as one candidate, not assume it's already true.

### The horizon-anchoring model (`PlanHorizon.cs`, `PlanHorizonCalculator.cs`)

`PlanHorizon` is either `Anchored(DateOnly raceDate, int targetTotalWeeks)` (a future target event inside the plannable ceiling — the plan's `TotalWeeks` is forced to equal the race week) or `NoAnchor()` (no event, an event in the past/current week, or an event beyond the plannable ceiling — the general-fitness case). Both branches still produce a macro plan with a concrete `TotalWeeks`; `PlanHorizon.NoAnchor()` itself just carries no `TargetTotalWeeks` to force it to a specific number.

### The rolling plan horizon (DEC-090, just locked — `docs/decisions/decision-log.md` § DEC-090, full design at `docs/plans/plan-horizon/rolling-horizon-plan.md`)

A new `PlanHorizonSweeper : BackgroundService` ticks every 30 minutes and keeps meso+micro populated 2 weeks ahead of the runner's date-derived current week, **up to `macro.TotalWeeks` and never past it** — extension is a pure `SelectNextIncompleteWeek` selecting the lowest week missing meso or micro, capped at `min(currentWeek + BufferWeeks, macro.TotalWeeks)`. It appends `MesoCycleCreated`/`MicroCycleCreated` events directly to the plan's existing Marten stream; it never constructs a new `PlanEventSequence` (that stays scoped to the two existing `StartStream` call sites: onboarding completion and `POST /plan/regenerate`). A deterministic, non-LLM `TrainingHistorySummary` (adherence rate, current adaptation-signal state, restructure count, the already-adapted ground-truth weekly target) is threaded into each extension call via suffix-only prompt additions, so extension reasons from real history rather than just the frozen onboarding-day snapshot. **Hard invariant: no LLM call this design adds ever runs inside a user-facing request** — every new call happens inside the sweeper's own background tick, isolated per-plan, with a 5-consecutive-failure circuit breaker.

This closes the gap for **anchored** plans cleanly: the sweeper fills meso/micro up to race week, and the race itself is the natural terminus — nothing more is needed. It does **not** close the gap for **NoAnchor** plans: `macro.TotalWeeks` is some number the LLM picked at generation time (there is no equations-based lower bound the way there is for pace zones — this is a coaching-judgment number, not a computed one), and once the sweeper has filled every week up to it, a NoAnchor runner who is still training has nothing left to extend into.

### The current placeholder (explicitly named as a placeholder, not a decision)

`WeekContext.FromMacro` (`backend/src/RunCoach.Api/Modules/Training/Plan/WeekContext.cs:32-67`) derives per-week phase context by walking `macro.Phases` and summing each phase's declared `Weeks`. When the requested week index exceeds the sum of all declared phases, it falls through to `return new WeekContext(weekIndex, lastPhase.PhaseType, IsDeloadCandidate: false)` (lines 65-66) — the last declared phase, unchanged, forever. This means a NoAnchor plan rolling past its declared `TotalWeeks` today would keep generating plausible-shaped weeks (the sweeper's `SelectNextIncompleteWeek` is capped at `macro.TotalWeeks`, so in practice extension simply *stops* there — the fallback is what protects any code that asks for `WeekContext` at an out-of-range index, a defensive guard, not an extension mechanism) — it degrades **gracefully** (nothing crashes, nothing renders blank) but not **scientifically** (there is no mechanism today that makes week 21 of a NoAnchor plan meaningfully different from week 20, or that ever revisits the runner's now-months-old onboarding-day phase plan). This is accepted as a named, temporary placeholder in DEC-090 specifically pending this research artifact — it is not the thing being defended or extended; it's what gets replaced.

## Research Question

**Primary:** For a NoAnchor RunCoach plan whose rolling horizon has reached the macro tier's last declared week and whose runner is still training, what should the system do next — and how should that decision be implemented against RunCoach's actual event-sourced plan model (Marten streams, the `PlanEventSequence` contract, the DEC-090 sweeper) rather than in the abstract?

**Candidate directions to evaluate (this list is a starting point, not exhaustive — find and evaluate others if the literature or prior art suggests them):**

1. **Re-run the macro tier** for a fresh training block once the current one completes, seeded by what actually happened during the completed block (adherence, current fitness signal, what phases ran, any restructures) — analogous in spirit to the DEC-090 `TrainingHistorySummary` graft already built for meso/micro extension.
2. **Extend `TotalWeeks` in place** and keep periodizing forward within the existing macro — i.e., the macro plan itself grows rather than terminating and restarting.
3. **Cap the rolling window at a fixed horizon regardless of `TotalWeeks`** — the plan conceptually never "ends" for a NoAnchor runner; the sweeper always keeps some fixed number of weeks populated ahead, and "TotalWeeks" stops being a meaningful ceiling for this plan type at all.
4. **Something else** — in particular, investigate whether real periodization practice treats indefinite non-race training as a sequence of **repeating blocks** (each with its own micro-goal or theme), a single **long-running maintenance phase**, or something structurally different from both "extend" and "restart."

**Sub-questions the research must answer to ground a recommendation:**

1. **How do real coaches structure indefinite, goal-less training?** Survey exercise-physiology and coaching-periodization literature and practice for runners who train continuously without a target race — recreational/lifetime runners, "just stay fit" populations, off-season/general-physical-preparedness (GPP) blocks between racing seasons. Does periodization theory (Bompa, Issurin's block periodization, Verkhoshansky, Daniels' own guidance for non-racing periods, Pfitzinger, the Hansons, 80/20/polarized-training literature) have a real answer for "no discrete peak," or is periodization theory itself fundamentally built around a peak/taper and this is a genuine edge case even in the source literature? If it's a genuine edge case, say so plainly rather than forcing a fit.
2. **Do training blocks repeat, and does a "maintenance phase" concept exist and hold up?** Is there a real distinction in the literature between a maintenance phase (sustained fitness, no progressive overload) and a repeating base→build→(mini-peak)→recovery cycle for goalless training? Which is more common in real coaching practice for recreational distance runners with no calendar race? Does "maintenance" deserve to be a first-class `PhaseType` in RunCoach's model (see the enum gap noted above), or is that the wrong abstraction?
3. **What do informal peaks / benchmarks do for goalless training?** Some coaching practice for non-racing runners still uses periodic time trials, benchmark efforts, or informal "focus blocks" (speed emphasis, strength block, aerobic base emphasis) as pseudo-peaks that create rhythm without a race. Is this a better mental model than "no anchor at all," and if so, does it change which candidate direction is correct?
4. **What do comparable products actually do?** Survey how existing AI/algorithmic training-plan tools (TrainingPeaks, Garmin Coach, COROS Training Hub, Runna, TrainAsONE, Whoop, any other adaptive running/endurance coach) handle a user with no goal race — do their plans ever "end," get silently regenerated, extend indefinitely, or degrade to something simpler? This is a competitive/product-precedent question, not just an academic one — R-002's competitive-landscape research (`docs/research/artifacts/batch-1b-competitive-landscape.md`) may already have partial answers worth checking before treating this as fully open.
5. **Event-sourcing consequences — does a new block mean a new Marten stream, a new event on the existing stream, or something else?** RunCoach already has one precedent for "a plan restarts": `POST /plan/regenerate` (`RegeneratePlanHandler.cs`) starts a **fresh** Marten stream with a fresh `PlanStartDate` in response to a user action (goal change, major life event). Should NoAnchor rollover reuse that exact mechanism (a new stream, sweeper-triggered instead of user-triggered), or does closing out and starting a new stream lose something a same-stream continuation would preserve? If a new-block decision instead appends to the **existing** stream, note that `PlanEventSequence`'s hard equality guard (`Mesos.Count == ExpectedMesoCount`, `ExpectedMesoCount = 4`) validates the canonical `[PlanGenerated, MesoCycleCreated×4, FirstMicroCycleCreated]` shape and is — per DEC-090 D4 — deliberately scoped to only the two `StartStream` call sites; a same-stream "new macro block" would need either a new event type that doesn't run through `PlanEventSequence` at all (the DEC-090 precedent for meso/micro extension) or an explicit, justified change to that validation contract. Work out which fits, and sketch the concrete event/projection shape (new event type(s), `PlanProjection.Apply` overload(s), whether `MacroPlanOutput`/`Phases[]` becomes an append-only list of blocks rather than one fixed array).
6. **What happens to the runner's history and adaptation state across the boundary?** `AdaptationSignalState`, restructure count, the already-adapted ground-truth weekly target, and the workout-log history all currently live against one plan/stream. If a new block means a new stream (regenerate-style), does adaptation state carry forward, reset, or get summarized into the new block's seed (the way DEC-090's `TrainingHistorySummary` already does for meso/micro extension within a stream)? If it means extending the same stream, this question mostly disappears — say so explicitly if that's a deciding factor in favor of same-stream continuation.
7. **What does the UI show at the seam?** `docs/plans/split-ui-cycle/slice-2-today.md`'s Today header renders `WEEK N OF M` off `resolveCurrentWeek`, which DEC-090 already makes converge on the runner's real current week. At a NoAnchor rollover boundary, does that counter reset to "Week 1" of a new block, keep counting the runner's total weeks trained, or does something else need to change? Does the runner get any signal at all that a new block started (a coaching-voice narrative moment, consistent with the persona in `docs/planning/coaching-persona.md`), or should this be invisible plumbing exactly like the existing weekly rolling extension is? Keep this recommendation at the level of "what data/event the frontend would need," not a visual design pass — the SPLIT/Alpine visual system is out of scope here.
8. **How does the new-block trigger integrate with the already-locked DEC-090 sweeper mechanics?** DEC-090's D1-D8 (timer-only trigger, 30-min cadence/2-week buffer, lowest-incomplete-week selection, idempotency via projection-state-as-source-of-truth plus a disposable marker, the `TrainingHistorySummary` graft, no-synchronous-fallback failure handling, required-but-not-backported meso validator) are locked and **must not be re-litigated** — treat them as fixed constraints. The open question is narrower: when and how does a NoAnchor plan's rollover past `macro.TotalWeeks` get *detected and dispatched* — is it the same `PlanHorizonSweeper` tick noticing `currentWeek` is within `BufferWeeks` of `macro.TotalWeeks` on a NoAnchor plan and dispatching a new command/handler, or does it need its own trigger? Same "never runs inside a user-facing request" invariant applies — confirm the recommended design honors it.
9. **Cost and scope discipline.** DEC-090's quality requirement bounds any single plan's extension spend at `macro.TotalWeeks × 2` LLM calls, with zero further spend once a runner stops training. Whatever rollover mechanism this research recommends needs an equivalent bound — an indefinitely-training NoAnchor runner should have predictable, not runaway, LLM spend per block/extension, and a runner who stops training should stop costing anything within one buffer window, same as today.

## Why It Matters

This is the one explicitly-deferred open question from DEC-090 (`docs/decisions/decision-log.md` § DEC-090, Consequences): *"NoAnchor... plan rollover past the last declared week is a genuine open question and is explicitly not decided here... this ship uses `WeekContext.FromMacro`'s existing last-phase fallback as a named, degrades-gracefully-not-scientifically placeholder until that artifact lands."* Every other piece of the rolling-horizon design (D1-D8) is locked and shipping independently of this research — **this research is not blocking that work**. But every NoAnchor runner (RunCoach's own stated MVP-0 audience skews toward this — self+family use, not exclusively racers) will eventually hit this seam in production, and the placeholder is explicitly not a real answer: it produces indefinitely-repeating stale-phase training, which is precisely the "static plan generator... that never adjusts" anti-pattern `docs/planning/vision-and-principles.md` opens by rejecting as the competitive non-differentiator. Getting this right is directly load-bearing for the product's stated thesis that the coaching intelligence "reasons about [training history] continuously," not just within a single fixed-length plan.
